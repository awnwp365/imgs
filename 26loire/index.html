🏰 Choose Your Adventure — Pick One (or Two!)

⭐ Top Pick for Families 📍 ~10 km · 15 min

See All the Châteaux in One Go

Why visit one château when you can see 41? The Parc des Mini-Châteaux presents over 40 iconic Loire Valley castles at 1:25 scale — so detailed you'll feel like a giant strolling through history. Kids can ride mechanical horses in knight outfits, drive mini-tractors, and hunt for treasure. A brilliant warm-up to see which châteaux to put on the must-visit list.

Book Tickets
🏆 The Grande Dame 📍 ~66 km · 1 hr 5 min

Chambord: Where Kings Played

François I's legendary hunting palace is Loire Valley royalty — the largest château of them all, with 440 rooms, a double-helix staircase attributed to Leonardo da Vinci, and an estate the size of Paris. Wander the rooftop terraces, explore the vast grounds and woodland, and feel the grandeur of Renaissance France at its most extravagant. Allow a full half-day — it's worth every minute.

Visit Chambord
🧠 Kids Love This 📍 ~10 km · 15 min

Château du Clos Lucé — Leonardo's Last Home

At the invitation of King François I, Leonardo da Vinci spent the final three years of his life at this elegant manor just a stroll from the Amboise royal château. Kids go wild for the interactive section: full-size working models of his inventions are scattered through the magical garden — they can step inside his tank, cross his drawbridge, and operate his machines. The basement is packed with 3D models of his genius designs. History has never been this hands-on.

🎠 Pure Fun 📍 ~17 km · 20 min

Lulu Parc — Outdoor Adventure Park

An outdoor leisure park in Rochecorbon, near Tours, designed entirely for family fun. Bouncy castles, water games, pedal go-karts, a toddler play area, crazy golf, pony rides and a traditional guinguette serving food and cold drinks. It's open-air bliss for little ones who want one last big burst of energy before the drive home. A relaxed, happy, no-stress way to round off the weekend.

☀️ Afternoon
🪨 Unique Experience 📍 ~26 km · 30 min

TROGLO DEGUSTO

Tucked between Château de Chenonceau and Zoo de Beauval, Troglo Degusto opens the doors to a vast troglodyte world with 1,000 years of history. Explore fascinating underground galleries where geology, tradition and traces of the past intertwine — then round off the visit with a tasting of AOC wines from the Domaine des Tabourelles. During school holidays, special adventures and animations are laid on in the galleries for all ages. A truly unique oenotourism experience blending heritage, culture and gastronomy.
